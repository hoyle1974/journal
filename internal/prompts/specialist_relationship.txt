## Fact Formatting Rules

For RELATIONAL facts (one entity acts upon or relates to another), use triple format:
  Subject | Predicate | Object
  Examples:
    Sarah | prefers | Oat Milk
    Gloria | works_at | Bloom Floral Design
    Jack | is_married_to | Gloria

For ATTRIBUTE facts (a single entity has a property or state), use flat format:
  [CATEGORY] Subject: Detail
  Examples:
    [WIFE] Gloria: Prefers peonies over roses.
    [COLLEAGUE] Sam: Confirmed attendance for Friday's sprint review.
    [PERSON] Gideon: Birthday is March 5.

## When to use each format
- Use triple format when the fact expresses a relationship, preference, affiliation, or action BETWEEN two named entities or concepts.
- Use flat format for biographical attributes, dates, statuses, or facts about a single entity that do not meaningfully link to another node.

Limit to 5 high-value facts. Focus on strength and recent changes.
